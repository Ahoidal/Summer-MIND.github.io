Who can apply?

We welcome applications from graduate students, postdocs, and junior (untenured) faculty.

What are the costs?

There is no application fee. If your application is accepted and you decide to attend, you will be responsible for your own travel to and from Hanover, NH (see here for some options). We will take care of everything else, including lodging and meals.

Application procedure

Apply by e-mail to summermind@gmail.com by April 15, 2017, 11.59pm EST.

Your application should consist of a single PDF file, which includes (1) a statement of intent (one page), describing how what you hope to learn or accomplish fits your academic goals, and (2) a current CV.

In addition, please arrange to have one letter of recommendation sent to the same address.

Why Dartmouth?

The three levels of analysis central to the course are directly reflected in the expertise of the organizers: hippocampal circuits that support learning and memory (van der Meer), full-brain network interactions that support high-level cognition (Manning), and interpersonal and group dynamics that support social interactions (Chang), all recent hires in the department of Department for Psychological and Brain Sciences. This department has a rich history in pioneering innovations in Cognitive Neuroscience: in 1988, Michael Gazzaniga and colleagues began the Summer Institute in Cognitive Neuroscience, which moved to UCSB in 2007. In 1999, Dartmouth was the first academic psychology program to operate its own research-dedicated MRI scanner, which was a key aspect to training at the Summer Institute. The department has organized regular summer workshops in computational cognitive neuroscience. Beyond the department, Dartmouth has a strong active interdisciplinary community interested in computational science coordinated by the Neukom Institute for Computational science, which sponsors many speaker series and workshops, including a network science workgroup.
